Types of Hot Coffee Drinks

Black Coffee :

No frills here: Black coffee is made from plain ground coffee beans that are brewed hot. It's served without added sugar, milk, or flavorings.

Decaf :

Coffee beans naturally contain caffeine, but roasters can use different processes to remove almost all of it. Decaf coffee is brewed with these decaffeinated beans.

Espresso :

A single espresso is a one-ounce shot. It's also the base for popular coffee-shop drinks like lattes and cappuccinos.

Latte :

This classic drink is 1/3 espresso and 2/3 steamed milk, topped with a thin layer of foam, but coffee shops have come up with seemingly endless customizations.

Cappuccino :

This drink is similar to a latte, but the frothy top layer is thicker.The ratio is equal parts espresso, steamed milk, and foam. It's often served in a 6-ounce cup.

Macchiato :

A macchiato is a shot of espresso with just a touch of steamed milk or foam. In Italian, macchiato means "stained" so a caffè macchiato refers to coffee that's been stained with milk.

Cortado :

This drink, which hails from Spain, is half espresso, half steamed milk. Unlike many Italian coffee drinks, it contains little to no foam. It's typically served in a 4.5-ounce glass.

MENU

IRISH COFFEE

Irish coffee is a caffeinated alcoholic drink consisting of Irish whiskey, hot coffee, and sugar, stirred, and topped with cream.

Dalgona Coffee

Dalgona coffee is a beverage made by whipping equal parts instant coffee powder, sugar, and hot water and then adding it to cold or hot milk.

MACCHIATO

Macchiato, sometimes called espresso macchiato, is an espresso coffee drink with a small amount of milk, usually foamed.

COLD BREW

Cold brew is simply coffee that has been brewed with cold rather than hot water and usually involves a long steeping process—anywhere.
